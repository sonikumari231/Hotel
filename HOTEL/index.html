Room Types

Single Occupancy Room

A room assigned to one person.May have one or more beds. A single room in a hotel is a type of accommodation that is designed for one person.


Family Room

A Parlour or living room connected with to one or more bedrooms. Family rooms are typically larger than standard rooms and can vary in size depending on the hotel's location, star rating, and price point.


Duplex Room

A duplex room in a hotel is a type of accommodation that is designed as a two-level living space within a single room. The lower level of a duplex room usually includes a living area with a sofa, armchairs, and a coffee table, as well as a workspace and a bathroom. The upper level typically contains a sleeping area, which may include a double or queen-sized bed, and additional seating or storage areas.
